# Videomaddness Showcase Video Script
**Target Duration:** ~3 - 4 Minutes  
**Video Style:** Fast-paced, high-tech, glitchy cyber-noir aesthetic. Heavy use of sync cuts matching visual glitches to low-end bass drops or synthesizer sweeps in the soundtrack.  
**Background Track:** Aggressive synthwave, industrial techno, or dark ambient electronic track.  

---

## Part 1: Introduction (0:00 - 0:20)
*   **Visual:** Black screen. Dynamic glitch scanlines tear across the viewport. Rapid-fire text transitions flash: `VIDEOMADDNESS // INITIALIZING MOTION STACK...`. Suddenly, a colorful, infinite zooming kaleidoscope explosion fills the center canvas, zooming continuously.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Welcome to Videomaddness—a chaotic motion machine designed to turn static images into breathing, infinite-zooming digital art, pixel-sorted glitches, and audio-reactive loops. Let's dive into the control deck."

---

## Part 2: Source Images Deck & Core Masking (0:20 - 0:50)
*   **Visual:** Screen shifts to show the left sidebar. The cursor drags and drops 3 high-contrast artwork images into the **Source Images Deck**. The images list expands instantly. The cursor clicks on different images, showing how the loaded thumbnail updates.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Everything starts at the **Source Images Deck**. Drop in JPG, PNG, or WebP images to build your layer database. You can upload multiple files to trigger random image switches inside the depth loop."
*   **Visual:** Show the **Layer Isolation** panel expanding. The cursor clicks the **Mask Mode** dropdown and highlights each option:
    1.  **Adaptive (Equal Pixel Count)**: Automatically splits the image into layers containing equal numbers of pixels for balanced depth detail.
    2.  **Luminosity (Brightness)**: Splits the image based on brightness levels, isolating highlights and shadows.
    3.  **Color Range**: Selects specific hue coordinates for colored highlight separations.
    4.  **Randomized Bands**: Generates chaotic random boundary slices.
*   **Visual:** Move sliders for **Number of Layers** (showing depth stack growing from 3 to 10), **Layer Blending (Feather)** (softening mask overlaps), and **Layer Edge Fade (Vignette)** (which blends layer borders smoothly).
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Isolate detail boundaries using **Layer Isolation**. Choose between luminosity, hue color range, or equal-pixel adaptive distributions. Fine-tune overlaps with feather controls and use **Layer Edge Fade** to feather the outer borders into a seamless composition."

---

## Part 3: Layer Inspector & Depth Matrix (0:50 - 1:15)
*   **Visual:** Expand the **Layer Inspector** section. Click on thumbnails in the **Extracted Layers** list. The preview canvas displays the isolated layers on checkerboard, black, white, and bright magenta backgrounds.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Inspect isolated masks individually inside the **Layer Inspector** on multiple checkerboard and solid backing colors to check the transparency levels and pixel densities before rendering."

---

## Part 4: Symmetrical Matrix & Infinite Motion (1:15 - 1:45)
*   **Visual:** Focus shifts to the right panel. Adjust **Diving Speed** (making the zoom accelerate forward and reverse) and **Depth Spacing** (changing the gaps between zooming cards).
*   **Visual:** Adjust **Rotation Speed** and click the **Reset Rotation** icon to snap it back to 0. Dial up the **Drift Speed** to show the camera wandering organically off-center.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Animate the stack inside the **Infinite Motion** panel. Control zoom speeds, adjust layer depth spacing, and introduce organic camera drift. Set continuous rotations and reset them instantly with a single button."
*   **Visual:** Expand **Symmetrical Matrix**. Switch **Mirror Symmetry** between Horizontal, Vertical, Quad, and Kaleidoscope. Dial the **Radial Slices** slider from 3 to 24 slices, showing the canvas morphing into a complex shifting mandala.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Activate the **Symmetrical Matrix** to duplicate and mirror your compositions. Warp depth cards using Horizontal, Vertical, or Quad mirrors, or dial up the Kaleidoscope radial slices to generate beautiful geometric mandalas."

---

## Part 5: Cybernetic Glitch FX (1:45 - 2:20)
*   **Visual:** Toggle on **Glitch FX Enabled**. The screen starts twitching. 
*   **Visual:** Show checkboxes for **Stackable Glitch Styles**:
    1.  **RGB & Pixel Sort**: Displaces red/blue values and sorts pixels horizontally.
    2.  **Analog VCR Scanline Sag**: Adds wavy analog distortions.
    3.  **Digital Block Tearing**: Tears the image into rectangular mosaic fragments.
    4.  **Liquid Warp Filter**: Generates GPU-accelerated coordinate warps.
*   **Visual:** Check multiple boxes to show them layering. Toggle the **Monochrome Glitch FX** checkbox to turn the splits and sorting into stark grayscale values. Adjust **RGB Displacement**, **Pixel Sorting Intensity**, **Shake Chance**, and **Shake Intensity** sliders.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Inject digital decay with stackable **Cybernetic Glitch FX**. Layer chromatic split offsets, analog VCR sag, digital block tearing, and GPU liquid warps. Push them into high-contrast grayscale with **Monochrome Glitch** or randomize styles on visual spikes."

---

## Part 6: Multi-Track Timeline & Soundtrack (2:20 - 3:00)
*   **Visual:** Camera moves down to the bottom **Loop Timeline**. Add text using **Add Text (New Track)** and **Add Text (Same Track)**. Double-click a text block to show text settings.
*   **Visual:** Modify Text parameters: **Size**, **Angle**, **Glitch Style** (RGB split, matrix scramble, flicker, chaos), **Idle Jitter**, and **Glitch Twitch** (making the text warp discretely). Click the **In/Out Transition** dropdown (Fade & Blur, Scale Zoom, Slide Glitch, Character Scatter, Glitch Reveal).
*   **Visual:** Drag a text block horizontally to seek time, edge-resize it to shorten, and drag it vertically to swap track index depths. Click the **Duplicate** button.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Arrange text and media elements along the **Loop Timeline**. Adjust duration, overlap blocks, and duplicate tracks instantly. Apply discrete idle jitters, rotation twitches, and select premium transition effects like Slide Glitch or Character Scatter."
*   **Visual:** Click **Add Graphic** and select an overlay logo image. Adjust start/end times and scale. Click **Add Audio** and upload a song file. The timeline renders an interactive **Audio Waveform**. Drag the block to adjust start offset.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Drag and drop graphic overlay layers with individual timeline bounds. Upload audio soundtracks to render interactive waveforms and synchronize sound directly with the loop boundaries."

---

## Part 7: Background Videos & Timeline Glitch Triggers (3:00 - 3:40)
*   **Visual:** Click **Add Video Background** and upload a video file. The timeline renders a video track, and the video plays behind the zooming deck.
*   **Visual:** Click **Add Glitch Trigger (⚡)** in the timeline header. Since Glitch FX is off, show the button is dimmed and disabled. Turn on Glitch FX in the sidebar; the button lights up pink. Click it to drop a trigger block.
*   **Visual:** Drag the trigger block. Select it to show the **Glitch Trigger Settings** panel (adjust trigger time, duration, and severity). Play back the timeline: as the playhead crosses the marker, the canvas executes a perfect frame-locked glitch explosion.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Need a background video? Load one directly behind your depth stacks. Keep interactions clean with smart UX button states: timeline **Glitch Triggers** remain dimmed until Glitch FX is activated. Pin triggers to exact frames to command glitch explosions at precise beats, complete with customizable durations and severity."

---

## Part 8: The Exporting Machine & Setup Guide (3:40 - 4:15)
*   **Visual:** Show the **Exporting Machine** panel. Select **MP4 Video** (WebCodecs H.264) or **WebM** formats. Select resolution and FPS, then hit **Export**. An overlay with a progress bar and frame counters fills the canvas, rendering frame-perfect outputs.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "When you're ready, deploy the **Exporting Machine**. Compile perfect-loop cycles or specific durations into high-quality MP4 or WebM files with frame-perfect seeking, ensuring zero audio/video drift."
*   **Visual:** Show a console terminal window on Windows. It double-clicks `run_Windows.bat` and launches the browser to `localhost:8000`. Next, a brief command block shows `docker compose up --build`.
*   **Voiceover / On-Screen Text:**  
    "Getting started is simple. Double-click the native launcher **`run_Windows.bat`** (or `run_Mac_Linux.sh` on Unix) to automatically start a local HTTP server. Or deploy the application globally using our pre-configured **Docker** Nginx environment.

    Unleash your visual chaos. Try Videomaddness today."
*   **Visual:** Visual fades out with the logo and GitHub URL: `github.com/honkamies/videomaddness`.
